## What you can do without asking

You may:

- **Say "Clipkit-compatible"** to describe an implementation, tool, or
  product that conforms to the Clipkit Protocol. Example:
  *"Foobar Render is Clipkit-compatible."*
- **Refer to Clipkit by name** in articles, blog posts, talks, tutorials,
  comparisons, books, podcasts, and similar editorial contexts.
- **Use the Clipkit logo** to link to clipkit.dev or to indicate that
  your project uses Clipkit (a "powered by" badge).
- **Quote the documentation** under the Apache License terms.

## What you can't do without permission

You may not:

- **Name your product, project, or service "Clipkit"** (or any
  confusingly similar variation such as "ClipKit", "Clip Kit", "Clipkit
  Pro", "Clipkit Cloud", etc.) without written permission.
- **Use the Clipkit logo as your own logo**, or modify it to create a
  derivative mark.
- **Imply official endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation** that does
  not exist. "Clipkit-compatible" is fine; "Official Clipkit Rendering
  Service" is not, unless that's literally true.
- **Use the marks in domain names** without permission (e.g.
  `clipkit-render.com`, `clipkitpro.io`).
- **Use the marks on merchandise** offered for sale without permission.

## How to ask

For commercial use of the Clipkit marks beyond the cases above —
including product names, hosted services that want to incorporate the
brand, and partnership scenarios — email **ianscott313@gmail.com**.

Requests for educational, community, or non-commercial use are usually
granted in good faith; commercial requests are evaluated case-by-case.

## Why this exists

A protocol becomes a standard when many people implement it. We want
that. The protocol is open under Apache-2.0 specifically so that anyone
can build a conforming implementation.

But "Clipkit" itself — the name, the logo, the brand identity defined
in the brand board — is how users tell official from unofficial. If
anyone can call any video renderer "Clipkit," the name becomes
worthless and so does the protocol's credibility.

So the protocol is maximally open. The brand is protected. The two
together let the ecosystem grow without diluting what makes Clipkit
recognizable.
